Neduntheevu (Delft Island)

Nedunateevu or neduntivu is a small island located in Jaffna, Sri lanka. The island was called as Delft island by the Dutch colonials. The island expands for about 50 sq. km. this flat and oval shaped island is usually wind-swept. After reaching to Kurikadduwan, you can take a ferry to the Delft Island. You can see a group of ponies scattering in the ground. The island is surrounded with shallow water and few kinds of aquatic fishes. Palmyra palm trees, dry shrubs and semi arid tropical type plants can be seen. To the west of the island you can see a ruined complex of south Indian chola Architecture. Jaffna Fort

Jaffna is a significant hot spot of Sri lankan tourism due to its archeological value. Jaffna fort is just one of the Jaffna's many landmarks to discover. This sheer beauty facing the Indian Ocean was an important place of trading industry in the colonial era. The original fort firstly built by Portuguese in 1618 and revamped by Dutch in 1680. The Portuguesa governor Pilip de Olivera initiated to construct the fort. Hence due to the invasion of Dutch it was captured by the dutch commander, raiclo van Goins. Again the fort had to be handed over to the Britain instead of the Britain invasion of Sri lanka in 1795. The Jaffna fort is concerned as the second largest remaining fort of Sri Lanka. Nallur Kovil

Nallur Kandasamy Kovil, so called as Nallur Murugan Kovil is an edifice dedicated to Lord Murugan. The Hindu shrine was constructed around 1749 by Krishna Suba lyer and Ragunatha Maapaana Mudaliyar. The adopted son of King Bhuvanekabahu, Sanpaha Perumal was initiated to build the monument. The route to the kovil is about 1.5 km from Jaffna on the point Pedro road. It has earned a reputation among Hindu devotees of Sri lanka since from the past. This ancient shine which represents the Hindu identity of North has a long history. It is said that the Nallur Kovil was founded in 948 and blossomed under the reign of Dravidian King kalinga Magha. Prince Sapumal who ruled the Jaffna Kingom also revamped the shrine several times. Nagadeepa Purana Vihara

Nagadipa Purana viharaya basking on the island Nainativu in Jaffna is one of the ideal places to be toured. The famous stupa was being worshipped since from the 1st century. Certainly the trip to Nagadipa Temple is like an adventure. The route located from Jaffna runs askew along causeway to the Kayts and another cause way to the Punkudativu. So you have to begin from Kurikadduwan pier at Pinudativu Island by a ferry. This Punkudative Island is linked by a causeway across the Palk bay to kayts. The total distance will be about 30 from Jaffna to Nagadipa. In accordance to the great chronicle 'Mahawansa', it is one of the most venerable places of Sri Lanka.

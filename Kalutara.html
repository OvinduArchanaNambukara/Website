Richmand Castle

Richmond castle one of the most beautiful spots, established in kalutara. A glimpse to the castle will be enough to understand the lavish lifestyle that the owner had. However you can explore and tour deep into the history of the castle with help of the caretaker in the place. Since from the entrance to the end of the castle gives you a vainglory feeling. This two storied mansion is located on a 42 acre fruit garden built in 1896.The main hall of the castle is designed with a natural air conditioning system by making a tunnel which mouths to the castle from the Kalu River. Kande Viharaya Temple

Kande viharaya temple located at Aluthgama is another tourist attraction that you may get astound of the tourism of sri lanka. Thousands of devotees flock daily to the temple and obey the rituals. The temple gets the name 'Kande vihara' as it is placed on top of a hill. The temple was constructed in 1734 and has become most venerated places in Sri Lanka. Locals identify the temple as Bentota kande viharaa too. While kande viharaya was founded by ven. Karapagala Dewamitta thero it was built under the guidance of ven. Udugama Chandrasara thero and Ven Dedduwa Dhammananda thero. The most important element is the gigantic Samdhi Buddha statue unveiled in 2006. This 48 m height statue is the tallest sitting Buddha statue in the world. Rankoth Viharaya Temple

Rankoth Viharaya made tremendous strides at a pace to visit, the largest & most influential monastery in Panadura, Sri Lanka. This serene premise is visited by many travelers considering the endowment sites of Sri Lankan Tourism. It is also popular as Galkanda Viharaya until it was upgraded in the name of 'Rankoth Viharaya'. The temple is built on a top of a rock boulder and can be seen from the far distance. The image house is enriched with depictions regarding the life of the Lord Buddha and many Jataka stories. The dwelling place of the monks was built around 1830 A.D. it is believed that the place has a history going back. Hence the preaching hall of the temple is a impressive, with Dutch architecture. Fa Hien Caves

Fa hien caves basking on the kalutara district are the oldest evidences for Mesolithic habitation in Sri Lanka. The cave gets the name due to the visit by the Buddhist monk and the explorer 'Fa hieyn' to ancient caves in 399 A.D. Thero 'Fa hien' was at residency for a while on his journey to the Adams peak. These are concerned as the largest cave complex in sri lanka. In addition you have to go for the Yatagampitiya village near the Bulathsinhala town of kalutara to see the caves. The cliff face where the caves are positioned is marvelous. So that, it is witnessed through the Radiocarbon examines hat they are now 33000- 4750 years old.

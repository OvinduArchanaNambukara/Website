Kurunegala

Kurunegala is a bustling market town and transport hub between Colombo and Anuradhapura, and Kandy and Puttalam. The town is not a destination in itself, but there are a few diversions nearby.

Yapahuwa Rock Fortress

The solid looking ancient rock fortress complex of Yapahuwa lying on the north western province of Sri lanka is truly an amusement. The fortress lies on a gigantic 90 meter high rock stands on the lowlands. The great king Buvanekabahu constructed the fortress on a huge granite rock in 1272. Since the Polonnaruwa kingdom was transferred to Yapahuwa in 1272 due to the South Indian invasions, the sacred tooth relic had to be secured. Yapahuwa was a strong rock fortress and a palace to the king. Soon after the death of King Buvanekabahu in 1284, Pandiyans of South India invaded Sri Lanka again. As a result of that they captured the sacred tooth relic and raised a devastating reign in Sri Lanka. Arankele Forest Monastery

Arankele forest monastery is usually attracted by both local and foreign tourists. It is the most appropriate place for the travelers whom are coming to sri lanka purposely to visit peaceful and quietude sites. You will be completely refreshed and relaxed with the first visit to the Arankele forest hermitage. It is listed as one of the premier forest hermitages in Sri lanka. The hermitage is located in Ganewatte of kurunegala district, Sri lanka. The name Arankele exaggerates about a hermitage where Arahants resided. It is witnessed that famous Buddhist monk, Arahat Malyadeva thero was accommodated here. Even today about 18 bhikkus are practicing old and new meditation methods in the hermitage. Ridi Viharaya

The Ridi maha viharaya,an area believed to have been part of the royal capital of Kurunegala lies about 20 km off from Kurunegala town. The viharaya gets the name Ridee Vihara or the Silver temple cause of the silver ore found by King Dutugemunu. The legend reveals that at that time King lacked money to build Great Ruwanweli maha seya in Anuradhapura. So he could finish his masterpiece with the silver ore found. The great chronicle Mahavansa and Thupawansa says that King Dutugemunu build the Ridee maha viharaya complex as a tribute in 2nd century. The caves of ridi viharaya contain remarkable frescoes and Dutch tiles outspread throughout the main cave. Ridi Viharaya of Ridigama is an important raja maha vihara in cultural triangle. Dewagiri Viharaya

Dewagiri viharaya, nesting at Bingiriya village, Kurunegala of Sri Lanka is one of the ancient temples in Sri lanka. History enthusiasts are welcomed to be visited the temple regarding wonderful tourist attractions in Kurunegala. It is located about 16 miles off from Chilaw or 16 miles off from hettipola on Kurunegala- Chilawa road. Afterwards you have to travel about 1.6 km into the village from the road in order to reach the temple. The temple retains a great history of ancient Sri lanka. While the temple dates back to the 3rd century, the traders who departed from the Chilaw boat harbor (Chilaw was formerly known as Sala Thota) had placed a casket of relics and worshipped every day. Panduwasnuwara

Panduwasnuwara, the oldest of the legendary capitals in Sri Lanka is arguably most interested among history lovers. The ancient city of Panduwasnuwara is an eminent landmark to be visited in addition to the Sri lankan tourism. Yet King Panduwasadewa (5th century BC) came to the throne and made his capital at Panduwasnuwara it was known as Upatissa Pura in King's age. Panduwasuwara is located within 27 km to the northwest of Kurunegala. The site expands for about 20 hectares with full of archeological founding and ruined monuments which dates back to the 12th century AD. Simultaneously Panduwasnuwara was used as a place to recover the Sacred Tooth relic from the enemies. The six meter wide moat and the fortified wall will welcome you at the entrance. Ras Vehera (Sesuruwa) Buddha Statue

Sasseruwa (RasVehera) Buddha statue is a monumental shrine in Halgamuwa village of Kurunegala district, Sri lanka. Ras vehera famed due to the monastery complex, giant Buddha statue carved on a rock and several rock caves. At the same time ruined dagobas, moonstones and stone inscriptions which dates back to the 2nd century B.C are found. To reach the 13 meter height Buddha statue you have to climb about three hundred steps upwards from the vihara steadily. The Bo tree placed in the temple terrace is one of the 32 saplings of jaya Sri maha Bodhi in Anuradhapura and was planted by King Devanampiya tissa (250-210 B.C). It is said that the whole temple surrounding got lit by colorful rays of light when the sacred tree was planted.

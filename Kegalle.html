Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

The famous tourist destination, Pinnawala elephant orphanage is a nursery and a breeding ground of wild elephants established in 1975 by the Sri lanka department of Wild life conservation. The entire land expands for 25 acres. Thus this lovely orphanage can be visited within 13 km to the North West from Kegalla town, Sabaragamuwa province of Sri lanka. The Pinnawala elephant orphanage is a home for about 88 elephants including 51 females and 37 males. Hence the orphanage has initiated to treat and look after the orphaned baby elephants, patriarchs and adult wild elephants that missed love, care and protection. Occasionally sequestered baby elephants from their herds are moved to the orphanage. Some of the baby elephants had lost their mother elephants due to accidents and poachers killings. Millennium Elephant Foundation

The millennium elephant foundation,. established in 1999 is another tourist attraction in Sri lanka. The foundation was originated in 1970s as a club concept elephant bath. Since it was a brainchild of Sam samarasingha (1939-1991) declared as a place of sanctuary and a nursery where elephants are well treated, by the World society for the protection of Animals (WSPA). The foundation is located about 2 km from pinnawela, on the karandupona - kandy road. Kegalla, Sri lanka. You will gain a rare opportunity to feed and bath the elephants. The working men will guide you to scrub the jumbos with a coconut shell. Perhaps you can enjoy an elephant ride. Berendi Kovil

Places like Berendi kovil, basking on the kegalle district attract many tourists to Sri lanka in addtion to the Sri lankan tourism destinations. The ruins of the Berendi kovil have been identified as the only monument of the existing ruins of Seethawaka era. The edifice is dedicated to God Bahirawa (Gani). entire site was excavated by the great archeologist H.C.P Bell. In addition to arrive the Kovil you have to travel along the Avissawella- Ginigathhena roadt ill you find the Sithwaka bridge. Passing the bridge, you are taken to the signing board erected by the Archelogical department. By the way the shrine was partly destroyed by the Portuguese during the colonial period lasted in Ceylon. Aluth nuwara dedimunda devalaya is a high ranked religious shrine among the popular tourist destinations around Kegalla district, Sri lanka. In order to visit the place you have to go to Mawanella town (few kilo meters from Kandy). Then you have to travel for the Hingula village.
